Asameena

Asameena Podcast is a series of audio stories that explore naming practices in Palestine, where children are named for villages destroyed, but with the ultimate hope of recovery. Each episode tells the story of a single name, the deep connections that bind people with land. Asameena is part of the To Be-Named Project funded by Experimental Humanities Collaborative Network at Bard College. The podcast Director & Presenter: Saida Hamad. Digital Commutations, Media & Arts program Head Al-Quds Bard College. In partnership with Radio Atheer & Goethe institute.

Saida Hamad is a seasoned Palestinian journalist, educator, advocate, and media producer, dedicated to promoting equality, gender empowerment, justice, and inclusive narratives. With a strong academic background, extensive professional experience, and a creative flair for media production, Saida Hamad has made significant contributions in addressing societal challenges and fostering positive change.

Completing a Master of Arts degree in International Studies with a concentration in Diplomacy from Bir Zeit University in 2015, Saida Hamad developed a deep understanding of the power dynamics and social implications at play in geopolitical negotiations. Saida’s thesis, "The Zionist Colonial Discourse in Oslo Accords and the Transformation of the Official Palestinian Discourse," showcased her expertise in analyzing complex geopolitical issues through a critical lens.

Saida Hamad further honed her skills and knowledge by obtaining a Bachelor of Arts degree in English Literature from Arizona State University in 1987, providing her with a solid foundation in the humanities and fostering her ability to bring a nuanced perspective to her work.

In addition to her academic pursuits, Saida has made significant contributions to the media industry as a journalist and media producer. As Chief Editor, Programs Director, and TV Presenter at Wattan TV from 2012 to 2015, she oversaw program coordination, conducted investigative reporting, and created impactful media content. Saida has a keen eye for storytelling and has produced thought-provoking TV programs such as "Beam of Hope," which exclusively addressed women's issues and rights in Palestinian society. her expertise in media production has allowed her to shape narratives, elevate marginalized voices, and shed light on important social issues.

As an educator, Saida has played a pivotal role in fostering critical thinking and media literacy among her students. Currently serving as a Lecturer and Head of Digital Communications & Media in the Humanities and Practicing Arts division at Al-Quds Bard (AQB) since 2019, she has designed and taught a diverse range of courses, exploring media production, gender representation, and social justice. Her expertise in media production allows her to provide practical insights and hands-on experiences to her students, empowering them to create impactful media content.

Beyond her teaching and journalism roles, Saida actively contributes to academic events, conferences, and workshops focused on equality, gender empowerment, justice, and media production. She has participated as a speaker, panelist, and workshop facilitator, sharing her expertise and experiences in utilizing media as a tool for positive social change.

Saida is commitment to equality, gender empowerment, justice, and inclusive narratives extends beyond her professional roles. She actively engages in media production projects, creating content that challenges societal norms, amplifies marginalized voices, and fosters dialogue on pressing issues. Saida’s dedication to using media as a platform for social transformation is reflected in her ongoing efforts and collaborations.

With her interdisciplinary expertise, commitment to equality and justice, passion for media production, and dedication to education, Saida continues to make a significant impact in addressing societal inequalities and promoting inclusive narratives. Saida’s unique combination of skills and experiences positions her as a valuable advocate, educator, and media producer in the pursuit of equality, justice, and positive social change.
